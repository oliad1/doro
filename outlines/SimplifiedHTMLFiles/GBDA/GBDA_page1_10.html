Course Description

GBDA 210:

In this course students will learn the basic principles and practices of user experience design and testing. Students will learn how to plan, implement, and interpret tests conducted on digital design applications, focusing both on functionality and on the quality of the user's interaction with these technologies.

View requirements for GBDA 210

Course Goals

This course is designed to teach the basic principles and practices of User Experience (UX) design for digital applications. The course will focus on tools and techniques for user experience design. Students will learn each step of the iterative design thinking process: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test.

Â 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain and justify the benefits and drawbacks of user-centered design
Carry out an iterative process-oriented user experience design project
Apply a variety of methods to involve the people who use an application in the design process
Define requirements for interfaces
Design an interface that meets the requirements
Evaluate the usability of the designed interface against the defined requirements
