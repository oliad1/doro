Course Description

GER 201:

This course continues the work of GER101/102. It offers practice in speaking, reading, and writing, with vocabulary building, grammar, and exercises in comprehension.

View requirements for GER 201

German 201 is an integrative language class for students with previous knowledge of German (such as 101/102). In 201, you will learn to understand and use a variety of German language forms and patterns in meaningful contexts. You will also learn more about cultural topics in German-speaking environments and improve your language learning skills. Above all, we hope you will enjoy discovering more about the German language and cultural topics and gaining insight into the experiences of speakers of that language.

Some of you may be curious to learn more about studying German at UW. You can always contact your instructor via email with any questions or comments. For more information about the German program, consult the Germanic and Slavic Studies webpage.

Learning Outcomes

In this course, you will:
learn to understand and use a variety of German language patterns in meaningful contexts, thus advancing your language and interaction skills,
improve your language learning skills,
hear/read about and discuss cultural topics in German-speaking environments, and
gain insight into the experiences of people living in German-speaking countries through engaging with different media and expressing yourself creatively in writing and visually.
In GER 201, you will also read a complete novel in German. In the process, you will:
develop your reading strategies in German,
encounter language that is specific to the back and forth of spoken interaction,
practice language structures and vocabulary in regular individual creative tasks, and
build a community of language learners through working together regularly on reading-related tasks.

Academic Integrity

In order to maintain a culture of academic integrity, members of the University of Waterloo are expected to promote honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility. See the Academic Integrity webpage for more information.

For GER 201 in particular, this means:
Doing your own work in German 201: The default in this class is that all graded work will be done individually, unless the instructor's permission is given in advance of an assignment/take-home project. If you are in doubt, please ask. Some basic rules for writing assignments:

  1. You are not allowed to use electronic and online translating services (e.g., Google translator) to complete assignments for this course, because they will not help you acquire the language. Work completed with the help of such programs is easy to identify.
  2. You are not allowed to consult with any student outside of your section on any assignment unless authorized by your instructor. If you work with a tutor on improving your language skills, remember that tutors (including German-speaking friends or partners) are there to help you do your work, not to do it for you. For instance, requesting an explanation for a specific grammatical structure from a tutor would be okay, while asking your tutor to read through your text to correct all mistakes s/he sees would not be okay. If you receive help with a text, you must turn in both your first draft and a final version, thus showing evidence of your unaided work and the results of the tutor's input.
  3. Plagiarism is a serious violation of your commitment to Academic Integrity at UW and will be treated as such. Always cite all sources you use for any work by carefully quoting material and providing full references. For general guidance in writing and in using citation styles, visit UW Academic Integrity Referencing and Research Skills, Purdue Owl on Academic Writing, or the UW Writing and Communication Centre.
